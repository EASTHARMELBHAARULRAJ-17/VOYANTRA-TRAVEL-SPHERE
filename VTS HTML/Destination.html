HIMACHAL PRADESH

Himachal Pradesh is a premier Himalayan state in North India renowned for its spectacular mountain ranges, colonial hill stations, and adrenaline-pumping outdoor sports like trekking, skiing, and paragliding. It attracts millions of visitors seeking both serene nature escapes and vibrant cultural experiences.

KERALA

Kerala Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala is a diverse southwestern Indian state featuring a 595 km Arabian Sea coastline, lush Western Ghats hill stations, and an intricate network of emerald backwaters. It offers travelers a unique blend of pristine beaches, wildlife sanctuaries, and rich cultural traditions.

RAJASTHAN

Rajasthan, India’s largest state, is globally renowned for its royal heritage, featuring eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites, magnificent Rajputana architecture, vast Thar Desert dunes, and vibrant cultural festivals. Create LifeLong Memories with Scenic Landscapes and Historic Architecture.

UTTAR PRADESH

Uttar Pradesh is a cultural and architectural powerhouse, home to the iconic Taj Mahal, spiritual hubs like Varanasi and Ayodhya, and ancient Buddhist sites like Sarnath. It features a rich blend of Mughal heritage, epic Hindu pilgrimage centers, and massive historical forts.

View Details
KANYA KUMARI
×

KANYA KUMARI

Kanyakumari, the southernmost tip of peninsular India, is a spectacular coastal destination in Tamil Nadu where the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean meet. Famous for its tranquil spiritual centers and striking monuments, it is the only place in India where you can watch both the sun rise and set over the ocean.

KODAIKANAL
×

KODAIKANAL

Kodaikanal, the "Princess of Hill Stations", is a lush retreat in Tamil Nadu’s Palani Hills at an elevation of 2,133 meters. It features a star-shaped artificial lake, cascading waterfalls, and misty viewpoints. Peak seasons (April-June) offer vibrant flower shows, while off-seasons bring peaceful, slow-paced exploration.

View Details
MADURAI
×

MADURAI

Madurai, the "Athens of the East", is a 2,500-year-old living museum of Tamil culture located on the banks of the Vaigai River. Famous for its majestic Dravidian architecture, vibrant textile markets, and world-renowned culinary scene, it serves as the ultimate cultural gateway just downstream from Kodaikanal.

View Details
OOTY
×

OOTY

Ooty (Udhagamandalam), the "Queen of Hill Stations", is a premier South Indian getaway nestled in the Nilgiri hills at an altitude of 2,240 meters. Famous for its lush tea estates, scenic toy train, and cool climate, it offers travelers a perfect mix of colonial heritage and breathtaking natural escapes.

View Details
THANJAVUR
×

THANJAVUR

Thanjavur, the "Rice Bowl of Tamil Nadu," is a vibrant cultural hub in South India located approximately 342 km from Chennai. Rising to prominence as the capital of the Chola Empire, it is world-renowned for its UNESCO World Heritage monuments, ancient bronze arts, and iconic, glittering gold-foil paintings.
